Frequency-Weighted Neural Kalman Filters

Dogan, A. H., Demirel, B. U., Holz, C. · International Conference on Robotics and Automation (IEEE ICRA) · 2026

Problem

The Kalman filter is optimal only when sensor noise is white and Gaussian. In practice much sensor noise is frequency-localized (colored) - concentrated in particular frequency bands - and under such noise the classical filter and even recent neural Kalman variants degrade.

Method

FW-NKF augments the Kalman recursion with a learnable IIR filter applied causally to the innovation (the residual between the observed and the predicted measurement), parameterized by learnable coefficients. It is trained with "spectral supervision": the observation model maps both the ground-truth and the estimated state back to measurement space, and a frequency-domain loss term (weighted by a hyperparameter lambda) guides the filter to reconstruct noise-free sensor signals - frequency-selective denoising of the innovations rather than the uniform gain of a classical filter.

Experiments

FW-NKF is evaluated on synthetic systems (the Lorenz attractor and a nonlinear pendulum) and on real-world tracking - EuRoC MAV IMU odometry and UWB-IMU human pose estimation - against the classical KF, KalmanNet and its Bayesian/recursive variants, the Recurrent Kalman Network, and an autoregressive KF, reporting MSE, NRMSE and R^2 over multiple seeds. It consistently outperforms these baselines: e.g. on Lorenz it reaches about 0.276 MSE / 0.999 R^2 (versus roughly 19.5 MSE for KalmanNet), and on EuRoC MAV about 0.035 MSE / 0.989 R^2.

Context

Work carried out with ETH Zürich's Sensing, Interaction & Perception Lab; accepted at IEEE ICRA 2026.
