Beyond subjectivity: Continuous cybersickness detection using eeg-based multitaper spectrum estimation

Demirel, B. U., Dogan, A. H., Rossie, J., Möbus M., Holz, C. · IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics · 2025

Problem

Cybersickness (dizziness, nausea in VR) is usually scored once, after the session, with subjective questionnaires collected in motion-restricted setups. Such labels are coarse and retrospective and cannot say when sickness rises during free interaction.

Signals

The method reads two passively recorded streams during natural VR use, scalp EEG and head motion, and estimates cybersickness continuously with no per-user calibration.

Multitaper spectral features

A single periodogram of a short EEG window has high variance. The multitaper estimate averages \(K\) orthogonal tapers (discrete prolate spheroidal sequences \(h^{(k)}\)): \[ \hat{S}(f) = \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k=1}^{K} \left| \sum_{t} h^{(k)}_t\, x_t\, e^{-i 2\pi f t} \right|^2. \] Averaging independent tapered estimates trades a little spectral bias for much lower variance, which is what makes band-power features usable on the short, artifact-prone windows real-time detection needs; dedicated steps suppress the motion artifacts that head movement injects into the EEG.

Result

A model maps these spectral and kinematic features to a continuous cybersickness level over time. To the authors' knowledge it is the first system to track cybersickness dynamically during free VR interaction rather than scoring it afterwards, with no user-specific calibration. The authors release a dataset of 16 participants (about two hours of EEG and head-motion recordings) and the source code. Published in IEEE TVCG, 2025; with ETH Zürich's Sensing, Interaction & Perception Lab.
