Beyond subjectivity: Continuous cybersickness detection using eeg-based multitaper spectrum estimation

Demirel, B. U., Dogan, A. H., Rossie, J., Möbus M., Holz, C. · IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics · 2025

Problem

Cybersickness - the dizziness and nausea many users feel in VR - limits adoption, yet it has mostly been measured with subjective post-immersion questionnaires collected in motion-restricted, controlled setups. Such labels are coarse, retrospective, and ill-suited to tracking how sickness builds up during natural interaction.

Approach

This work estimates cybersickness continuously and objectively from passively monitored signals: EEG together with head motion. EEG power is estimated with the multitaper spectrum method, which trades a little bias for substantially lower variance than a single-taper spectrogram - valuable for the short, noisy windows real-time use demands - and the pipeline adds specialized processing to counter the motion artifacts that VR head movement injects into EEG. The method needs no user-specific calibration or personalization, so it generalizes across users out of the box.

Contribution

It is, to the authors' knowledge, the first method to track cybersickness levels dynamically during free VR interaction rather than scoring it once afterwards. The authors release a dataset of 16 participants (about two hours of EEG and head-motion recordings in VR) together with source code, providing an objective, reproducible alternative to questionnaire-based assessment.

Context

Joint work with ETH Zürich's Sensing, Interaction & Perception Lab; published in IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics (TVCG), 2025.
